# Federal Holidays and Their Significance

Federal holidays are days when the U.S. government closes. These holidays honor important people, events, and values in American history. Understanding them helps you connect with American culture and prepares you for citizenship test questions.

## What Is a Federal Holiday?

Federal holidays are established by Congress. On these days:
- Federal government offices close
- Many businesses close or reduce hours
- Banks and post offices typically close
- Many workers get paid time off

States can add their own holidays, but federal holidays apply nationwide.

## Independence Day: July 4th

**What it celebrates:** America's declaration of independence from Britain in 1776

Independence Day is the most important civic holiday in America. On July 4, 1776, the Continental Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence.

**How Americans celebrate:**
- Fireworks displays
- Parades
- Barbecues and picnics
- Patriotic music and flags

### Test Question

**Q: When do we celebrate Independence Day?**
A: July 4

This is one of the most common citizenship test questions. Remember: July 4, 1776 was when independence was declared.

## Memorial Day

**When:** Last Monday of May

**What it celebrates:** Honors military members who died serving the country

Memorial Day began after the Civil War to honor fallen soldiers. It marks the unofficial start of summer in American culture.

**How Americans observe:**
- Visiting cemeteries and memorials
- Flying flags at half-staff until noon
- Moments of silence
- Parades

## Veterans Day

**When:** November 11

**What it celebrates:** Honors all who served in the U.S. military

While Memorial Day honors those who died, Veterans Day honors all veterans—living and deceased. November 11 marks the end of World War I in 1918.

**How Americans observe:**
- Thanking veterans
- Parades and ceremonies
- Special recognition for service members

## Labor Day

**When:** First Monday of September

**What it celebrates:** American workers and the labor movement

Labor Day honors workers who built America. It marks the unofficial end of summer.

**How Americans celebrate:**
- Final summer barbecues
- Parades
- Time with family

## Thanksgiving

**When:** Fourth Thursday of November

**What it celebrates:** Gratitude and the early history of America

Thanksgiving recalls when Pilgrims and Native Americans shared a harvest feast in 1621. It's one of America's most important family holidays.

**How Americans celebrate:**
- Large family meals
- Turkey, stuffing, and pumpkin pie
- Expressing gratitude
- Watching football

## Presidents' Day

**When:** Third Monday of February

**What it celebrates:** Presidents, especially Washington and Lincoln

This holiday honors George Washington (born February 22) and Abraham Lincoln (born February 12). It's officially called Washington's Birthday.

## Martin Luther King Jr. Day

**When:** Third Monday of January

**What it celebrates:** The civil rights leader Martin Luther King Jr.

This holiday honors Dr. King's work for civil rights and racial equality. He led peaceful protests and delivered the famous "I Have a Dream" speech.

### Test Connection

**Q: What did Martin Luther King Jr. do?**
A: Fought for civil rights / Worked for equality for all Americans

## Other Federal Holidays

| Holiday | When | Honors |
|---------|------|--------|
| New Year's Day | January 1 | Start of new year |
| Columbus Day | Second Monday of October | Christopher Columbus's arrival in Americas |
| Juneteenth | June 19 | End of slavery in America |
| Christmas | December 25 | Christian holiday |

### Juneteenth

Added as a federal holiday in 2021, Juneteenth commemorates June 19, 1865—when enslaved people in Texas learned they were free. It celebrates the end of slavery in America.

## Test Questions About Holidays

**Q: When do we celebrate Independence Day?**
A: July 4

**Q: Name two national U.S. holidays.**
A: Any two from the list above (Independence Day, Memorial Day, Labor Day, etc.)

For the second question, you can name any two federal holidays. Independence Day and Thanksgiving are easy to remember.

## Cultural Significance

Understanding holidays helps you connect with American life:

- **Independence Day** reflects pride in American freedom
- **Memorial Day and Veterans Day** show respect for military service
- **Thanksgiving** emphasizes family and gratitude
- **MLK Day** honors the ongoing work for equality

These holidays represent American values: freedom, sacrifice, family, and justice.

## Participating as a Citizen

As a new citizen, you'll experience these holidays differently. You might:

- Attend a naturalization ceremony on July 4th (many are held then)
- Thank veterans on Veterans Day
- Share Thanksgiving traditions with your family
- Reflect on freedom and equality on MLK Day

These celebrations become part of your American experience.

## Quick Study Tips

For the test, definitely know:
1. **Independence Day is July 4** — This is almost always asked
2. **Two holidays you can name** — Have your two ready

The most commonly named holidays are:
- Independence Day (July 4)
- Thanksgiving
- Memorial Day
- Labor Day

Pick two you can remember easily and practice saying them.

You're not just learning for a test—you're learning about the culture of your new country.
